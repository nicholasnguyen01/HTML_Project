Gaius Julius Arminius (Hermann)

The man who saved Germanic Europe

He is one of the reason people still speak Germanic languages today (English, German, Dutch, etc)

Here's an excerpt timeline of Arminius' Life:

  • c. 18 BCE - Arminius was born the eldest son of the Cherusci chief Segimer.

  • Most likely he and his brothers (after being raised in Rome as a hostage) fought beside the legions under Tiberius Claudius Nero, stepson of Emperor Augustus, suppressing the huge Pannonian and Illyrian revolts.

  • ~8 CE - Arminius was transferred to the Rhine to serve under Governor Publius Quinctilius Varus. Varus' mission was to turn Germania Magna (Greater Germany), the tribal territories east of the Rhine, into a full-fledged Roman province. Although the Cherusci had received federated status within the Roman Empire, to Arminius it was clear that his people were not treated as equals.

  • To defeat the legions, Arminius united the tribes. He would lure Varus and his legions into the Teutoburg Forest. There the difficult terrain favored Arminius' lighter-armed, quick and nimble Germanic warriors.

  • The Battle of the Teutoburg Forest took place.

  • News of the destruction of three legions reached Emperor Augustus along with the head of Varus, courtesy of Maroboduus. An irate Augustus shouted, "Quinctilius Varus, give me back my legions". In light of the disaster in the Teutoburg, the Clades Variana, Augustus abandoned the conquest of Germania.

"Arminius' victory in the Teutoburg forest kept the Germanic tribes free of Roman dominion. Centuries later, their freedom would make possible the emergence of the nations of Germany, France, and England."

--Ludwig Heinrich Dyck
